Standing at number 6B Staringstraat in Alkmaar, this house has a history of its own. It was built in 1680 hence the old school face look. Beyond that, it was proven to have protected some Jewish people looking for safety from the Germans hunting for them. There has been claims that, they successfully took refugee in this home for about 2 years living in the artic. Later on in the early 2000s, it was given a huge makeover for the inside interior to give it's now modern touch on the inside. With its incredible history, as pass down owners, we feel the honor and obligation to respect and appriciate this living space for what it did and protect it as much as it protected those innocent jewish people seeking refugee.
